WHO AM I

Hello there! My name is Paolo, and I have recently completed my academic journey at NID — Nuovo Istituto Design, based in Perugia.

During these years, I learned how to develop an idea starting from research, concept development, and context analysis, always aiming to create each project into a coherent working visual system. I had the opportunity to work on proper brands and companies, both individually and especially in groups, improving my ability to collaborate and adapt my design approach.

At the moment, my main areas of interest are UI/UX design and AI tools, two fields that allow me to combine creativity, digital experiences, and new ways of approaching the design process.

01 / GRAPHIC DESIGN
Featured Project

SOGLIE / THRESHOLDS

Biggest project of my year was SOGLIE / THRESHOLDS — my first public exhibition experience, developed together with my university colleagues during the Graphic Design III course at NID — Nuovo Istituto Design in Perugia, under the creative direction of Cristiano Bottino.

The project explores the concept of boundaries, understood as a space of relationship and transition between the individual and the community. The Biblioteca degli Arconi is the generating principle of the entire exhibition.

My theme: PROXIMITY & DISTANCE

Within the shared space, each individual emits their own energy, made of presence, gestures, words, and identity. These signals intertwine with those of others, creating connections, frictions, and temporary balances.

Seven groups for seven interpretations. This exhibition unfolds around the concept of transition.

02 / UI/UX DESIGN
App Design

APP myNID

The myNID app was studied, designed, and structured according to the needs of each student for each course, each teacher and their subjects, as well as the administration office, with the goal of making the experience fast and efficient.

During the development of the app, we based our work on the existing website where these information were originally available. We also created and shared a questionnaire with several students, teachers, and members of the NID staff, in order to understand their needs, pros and cons.

Website Design

NDA WEBSITE

The NDA (NID Design Award) website, is a hypothetical website designed to present the event in a clear, accessible, and immediate way.

The site allows users to discover the overall identity of the event, the winners from previous editions, the participants, the guests, and the different award categories, including "Proiecto" and "Career" which are nowadays a tradition for the NDA.

The website was developed on Webflow with a simple but precise design goal: to create a fluid, readable, and intuitive experience.

Final Year Project — App Design

JOURNYX

JOURNYX is an app I created designed to offer one of the most accurate and authentic review systems ever, allowing each user to review games in a personal and meaningful way.

The app is not limited to reviews — I also implemented a system that allows users to track their progress within each game, turning JOURNYX into a true gaming diary.

The app includes several unique features, but the most relevant one is the possibility to request help from other users, turning the app into a collaborative space based on shared gaming experiences.

Visual Identity

JOURNYX — BRAND

The visual identity of JOURNYX was studied and created by taking inspiration from existing gaming platforms and apps, especially in terms of colors, typography, and tone of voice.

The most personal part of the project was the creation of the name. "JOURNYX" comes from the word "journey", later transformed into "journy". The "X" has a double meaning: on one hand, it recalls the "X" on a pirate map, a symbol of a goal or destination to reach; on the other hand, it refers to the "X" button, a recurring and recognizable element in the world of video games.

03 / PACKAGING DESIGN

This year, I had my first approach to the world of packaging design, where I learned about different types of structures, packaging systems, and the main rules behind packaging and product presentation.

Packaging Contest — Vecchio Amaro del Capo

AMARO DEL CAPO: EDIZIONE FREDDO ASSOLUTO

The goal for the contest of the notorious Italian liquor brand Vecchio Amaro del Capo was to create a unique packaging with an object thought for social moments.

My proposal was Amaro del Capo: Edizione Freddo Assoluto, a packaging that included a custom branded ice tray, designed to create a distinctive drinking experience full of personality and style.

The box was designed using a color palette that reflects the contrast between the warmth of the liquor and the coldness of the serving ritual.

Label Redesign

URBANI TARTUFI

A small project I made for the Italian brand Urbani Tartufi: I redesigned the labels for one of their truffle oil bottles, giving them a more organized, elegant, clean, and modern visual identity.

The aim of the project was to improve the overall readability and hierarchy of the label, while enhancing the premium character of the product.

This work later developed into a second project, where I recreated the product through a 3D render, focusing on its presentation and visual impact.

Brand & Packaging from Scratch

VENENO SAUCE

VENENO Sauce is a branding and packaging project developed from scratch around the concept of a habanero hot sauce. The name "VENENO", comes from Spanish meaning "poison", chosen to express the product's strong, spicy, and intense personality.

The project included the creation of the brand identity, label design, bottle presentation, color palette, and packaging system. The bold red tones, sharp visual elements, and a clean structure aim to communicate energy, heat, and a distinctive premium character.
